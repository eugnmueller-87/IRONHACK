# Lab Summary

The biggest challenge was wiring two LLM providers together so the output of one becomes the input of the other while keeping cost and rate-limit guardrails in place. I solved this by building a thin `CostTracker` that accumulates token counts across both providers in a single session, and a `ask_with_fallback()` method that silently retries on the secondary provider instead of crashing. Mocking the HTTP and SDK calls in tests was initially tricky because the OpenAI and Anthropic clients are instantiated inside `__init__`, but patching at the class level with `@patch("llm_providers.OpenAI")` rather than at the instance level resolved that. Key learning: separating transport (API calls), business logic (pipeline orchestration), and cost accounting into distinct modules made every piece independently testable. For improvement I would add SQLite persistence so repeated article URLs are served from cache, cutting costs and avoiding redundant sentiment calls.
