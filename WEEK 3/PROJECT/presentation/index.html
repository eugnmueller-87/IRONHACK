Knowledge Base Setup

Two layers of context injected into every prompt — document_processor.py parses and serves both.

Primary Knowledge Base — company-specific
brand_voice.md — tone rules, forbidden phrases ("I'm excited to share"), post formula, format rules
background.md — 10yr procurement career at TeamViewer, Scout24, Delivery Hero; why I built this
linkedin_examples.md — 2 full example posts with structure annotations for style matching
methodology.md — content pillars, post types, signal-to-post workflow, what makes a post worth writing
Secondary Research Layer — industry context
hermes_signals_sample.md — structured market signals with procurement angles: NVIDIA, TSMC, Anthropic, Micron, CrowdStrike
supplier_landscape.md — 250 companies across 17 categories, tier rankings, content angles per category
Document Processing
document_processor.py — ingests all .md files, builds context blocks capped at 8,000 chars for prompt injection
knowledge_base.py — manager class with brand_voice_context() and signals_context() helpers
Primary KB — who you are, how you write, what you've written before
Secondary KB — live market data from Hermes, supplier landscape, signal history
Together — every prompt gets both layers, making output specific to you and to what's happening right now

LLM API Integration & Prompt Engineering Templates

Two Claude models with distinct roles — llm_integration.py wraps both, prompt_templates.py defines all templates.

LLM API Integration — two models, two jobs
Claude Haiku — fast, cheap. Signal classification (11 types), significance scoring, market briefs. Runs on every crawl cycle.
Claude Sonnet — powerful. LinkedIn post drafting, signal analysis, content calendar generation. Runs on demand.
llm_integration.py — single LLMClient class, model-aware methods: generate_linkedin_post(), generate_brief(), classify_signal()
Prompt Engineering Templates — 5 reusable templates
LINKEDIN_POST — injects KB context + signal, enforces brand voice rules, 150-300 words
MARKET_BRIEF — summarises a list of signals into a procurement intelligence brief under 200 words
SIGNAL_ANALYSIS — 3 structured questions: what happened, why it matters, what to do in 30 days
CONTENT_CALENDAR — generates a 5-post weekly plan using available signals + background context
UNIQUENESS_COMPARISON — structured side-by-side: Hermes system vs generic ChatGPT across 6 dimensions
All templates use {kb_context} and {signal} placeholders — the KB and live signal are injected at runtime, keeping templates reusable across any company or topic.

Content Generation Pipeline

Step 6
🔄
Iterate
