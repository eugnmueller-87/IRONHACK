# Lab Summary — Relevance Scoring and Rerankers

Reranking helped most on precise legal queries against the EU AI Act, where embedding similarity alone often surfaced topically adjacent but not directly responsive chunks (e.g., retrieving definitional articles when the question asked about penalties). The Cohere reranker consistently promoted the most answer-relevant chunks into the top-3 positions, producing noticeably more accurate and citation-rich GPT answers. LLM-based scoring via GPT also improved over baseline but was slower and more expensive per query, making it less practical at scale. For podcast transcripts — which are conversational and less structured — the reranking gain was smaller because embedding similarity already captured semantic intent reasonably well. The recommendation: use a dedicated reranker (Cohere or a cross-encoder) whenever your corpus is formal and dense (legal, medical, technical documentation), the cost of a wrong answer is high, or you are retrieving across heterogeneous sources where a single embedding space cannot capture all relevance signals equally well. Skip reranking for conversational or short-document corpora where latency matters more than precision.
